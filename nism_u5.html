Peer Response: Team scanning exercise

Thank you for sharing your scan results. They appear to be similar to the results of my individual scan and that of some of my team members. Your team located the hosting country to be the United states of America. This was the conclusion I had initially made during scanning. However, our team has debated the reliability of generic IP address locaters, as they are known to be fairly inaccurate. Kevin (team member) suggested to use the route naming convention, the complete route information and the hop times. Through this, we arrived at the conclusion that the website was actually hosted in Amsterdam- which was the location of one of the hops found whilst using the TRACERT command. My suggestion would be to carry out another in-depth search for the hosting country, with this information in mind and see if you and your team arrive at the same conclusion.

Identifying the appropriate governing body and associated regulations that apply to our assigned site

  • PCI DSS- Requirements for organisations to accept, store, process, and transmit cardholder data during credit card transaction (4 levels)
  • The 12 requirements of PCI DSS
  • GDPR- governs the way in which we can use, process, and store personal data. Ways in which to make websites GDPR compliant: HTTPS, Contact forms, Cookie notice Notifications for policy updates
  • The Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) Accessibility Regulations 2018
  • Electronic Commerce (EC Directive)- establishes legal rules that online retailers and service providers must comply with (within the EU)

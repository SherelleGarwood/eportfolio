Peer Responses

I really enjoyed your post detailing the security threats outlined within the ‘Compromising a medical mannequin’ article. As you mentioned in your post, a brute force attack against the Wi-Fi Protected Setup register PIN numbers using Reaver. Updating the firmware so that it protects against these vulnerabilities seems like the most useful mitigation technique, however, healthcare facilities seem to be reluctant to do this. Do you think this is possibility due to the cost of updated firmware?

Your post also discussed the use of HPING3 to compromise the network, causing a denial of service attack. Firewall and intrusion detection systems are a strong form of prevention against denial of service attacks. However, the systems being used in the medical facility are legacy systems with mass amounts of vulnerabilities. Do you think an overhaul of the network devices would be more useful than trying to implement protective measures on a flawed system?

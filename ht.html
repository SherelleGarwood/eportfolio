Exercise 8.6C

I initially performed an F-Test Two sample for Variances. The sample variances for the male and female incomes, were 233.129 and 190.176 respectively. The observed F test statistic is F= 1.23 (2 dp) with 60 and 60 associated degrees of freedom, which gives a two tailed p-value of p=0.436. The observed F ratio is therefore not significant as the variances between men and women do not differ.

I proceeded to perform a T-Test: Two-sample assuming equal variances. The obtained independent samples t= 3.268 with 118 degrees of freedom. The associated two-tailed p-value is p= 0.0014, therefore the observed t is significant at the 1% level. The difference in means in 8.68, evidencing higher incomes for males who are cardholders (Superplus Diamond card) than females.

Unit 9: Analysing Qualitative Data

Summary Measures Worksheet

Exercise 8.1B

The sample size for Diet B is n=50, meaning that 50 people used Diet B, the same as Diet A. The sample mean weight loss in 3.710 kg (3dp). The diet appears to be effective but not as much as Diet A. The sample standard deviation for Diet B is 2.769 kg. This means that the data is more dispersed in relation to the mean, than Diet A. This may indicate that Diet B data is less reliable when determining it's effectiveness.

Exercise 8.2B

The sample median weight loss for Diet B is 3.745 kg. The diet appears to be effective but less effective than Diet A, which has a sample median weight loss of 5.642 kg. The sample interquartile range of the weight loss for Diet B is 3.451 kg, which is higher than Diet A at 3.285 kg. This means that the data for Diet B has greater spread than Diet A. This is supported by the standard deviation.

Exercise 8.3D

There were 70 respondants for Area 1. 15.7% of respondants preferred Brand A, 24.3% preferred Brand B and 60.0% preferred some other brand of breakfast cereal. There were 90 respondants for Area 2. 21.1% of respondants preferred Brand A, 33.3% preferred Brand B and 45.6% preferred some other brand of breakfast cereal. In both areas, Brand B appears to be the preferable cereal out of the two brands. However, most respondants prefer another brand of breakfast cereal.

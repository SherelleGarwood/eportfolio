Unit 9: Analysing Qualitative Data

Collaborative Learning Discussion: Accuracy of Information (Summary Post)

Nutritional research is a prominent factor is the understanding of the impact of food of health. Therefore, it is crucial that researchers maintain objectivity and impartiality in their analysis of nutritional data.

In Jonathan’s response to my initial post, he detailed some of the potential legal ramifications of Abi not providing a full and objective report to the manufacturer (Callaghan, 2023). There is the possibility of Abi facing court proceedings by breaching clauses stated within the Fraud Act 2006, such as “intention to deceive and cause harm” (UK Government, 2006).

The International Life Sciences Institute released a series of directives for industry-financed research, the primary of which mandates that researchers should allow the data to guide their conclusions, rather than striving to attain any predetermined outcomes (IUFoST Scientific Information Bulletin, 2012). As per this instruction, it would be considered unethical for Abi to manipulate his investigative procedures with the intention of arriving at a favourable outcome for the Whizz manufacturer. Abi is responsible for his published research and analysis of his findings.

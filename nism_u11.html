Team Activity Debate

Team 1: It is our belief that the future of the Internet is based on content centric networking (CCN &/or NDN or COAST).

Content centric networking is a form of internet architecture aimed at making data accessible by content name. This helps to eliminate current IP difficulties by using a name rather than address when routing packets across a network.

One benefit of content centric networking is that it is more flexible and scalable than the current host-centric networks. This means that providers are able to distribute information at a rate which meets demand. CCN uses a push and pull-based mechanism meaning that personal content can be delivered to users in real-time.

One example of content centric networking is NDN architecture. This replaces IP packets with content chunks (Ding, Yan and Deng, 2016). An interest packet is with the content name is sent to providers and data packets are retrieved for the user. This means that data is able to be delivered faster and on a larger scale than previous architectures.
